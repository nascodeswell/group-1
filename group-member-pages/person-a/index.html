Nassirah Nelson

design · STEM · Innovation

I am a multidisciplinary engineer and STEM educator whose work is rooted in social practice. My research explores ways to recontextualize and teach STEM through the arts, specifically through creative technologies. I work across a variety of mediums and aim to create programs and products that foster inclusivity and connection through collaboration and play.

I am the founder of Sample Mode: Introduction to Sound Design, Beat-making, and Creative Music Technologies, a Berkeley Changemaker Course®. The class is designed to offer students space to explore and develop both their STEM and creative identities through the art of sampling, music production, design thinking, and digital fabrication.

I also serve as the Program Manager for the CITRIS Innovation Hub, which includes the Invention Lab, a community-centered makerspace located on UC Berkeley’s campus. In this role, I develop programs that support faculty, student, and community innovation by providing the knowledge, tools, and support needed to rapidly design and prototype novel interactive products.
